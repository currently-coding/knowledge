Let $k \geq 2$:
1. $dya(2n+a)$ = $dya(n)a$ for $n \geq 0$ and $a \in \{1,2\}$
2. $dya^{-1}(xa)$ = $2 \cdot dya^{-1}(x) + a$ for $x \in \{1,2\}^*$ and $a \in \{1,2\}$
3. $ad_k(kn+a)$ = $ad_k(n)a$ for $n \geq 0$ and $a \in \{1, ..., k\}$
4. $ad_k^{-1}(xa)$ = $k \cdot ad_k^{-1}(x)+a$ for $x \in \{1, ..., k\}^*$ and $a \in \{1, ..., k\}$
