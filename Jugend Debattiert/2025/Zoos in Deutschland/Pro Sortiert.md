**1. Conservation Efforts:**
- Zoos contribute to saving endangered species, like the Przewalski's horse (Leipzig Zoo) and the northern bald ibis (Heidelberg Zoo).
- Provide safe environments for breeding programs and reintroduce animals into natural habitats.

**2. Education:**
- Serve as educational centers, enhancing public understanding of wildlife and conservation.
- Studies (Goethe University Frankfurt) show zoo visits increase student interest in nature and conservation.

**3. Research:**
- Conduct valuable research on animal behavior, genetics, and health.
- Example: Leibniz Institute for Zoo and Wildlife Research in Berlin conducts key research on wildlife adaptability and conservation.

**4. Rescue and Rehabilitation:**
- Zoos act as sanctuaries for injured or orphaned animals.
- Example: Münster Zoo's International Centre for the Conservation of Turtles focuses on endangered turtle species.

**5. Economic and Tourism Benefits:**
- Zoos attract tourists, boosting local economies and creating jobs.
- Revenue supports conservation projects and other community services.

**6. Public Awareness and Advocacy:**
- Raise awareness of environmental issues and advocate for policy changes.
- Example: Zoological Society for the Conservation of Species and Populations (ZGAP) works on conservation programs and public awareness.

**Conclusion:**
Zoos in Germany play a vital role in conservation, education, research, rescue, and public awareness, making a strong case for their continued existence. While improving animal welfare practices is crucial, the multifaceted contributions of zoos support their ongoing importance.
